 Notes on what I'm learning and building in computer science and software engineering.

I'm a principal engineer with around 16 years of experience, mostly in systems where software meets constrained hardware — smartcard emulation, NFC, cryptography and information security, Android, Linux. I've shipped real-time systems where performance, reliability, and security all matter at once.
For most of that time I didn't write anything down. I learned by reading, by building, and by working through problems on the job. I want to start writing things down now, for myself first. It might also be useful for trading notes with other engineers along the way.

Most of the interesting compute today is happening in AI. I'm currently exploring the infrastructure and software systems that run modern AI workloads — GPU programming, inference optimization, distributed training etc.
